How your AI team works

We work in sprints — just like a real development team. We plan with Maja, execute with Karolina, and you talk to Anna.

01

Conversation and planning

You describe what you need. Maja (Chief of Staff) plans the sprint, proposes tasks with story point estimates, priorities, and deadlines. You approve or adjust.

Maja Wojciechowska — sprint planning
02

INTENT — we define the goal, not the task

Every task starts with an INTENT: what problem we're solving, what the outcome should be, and how we'll know it's a success. This way we verify not whether it was "done," but whether it was "solved."

problem: Customers wait 24h for a response
outcome: Automatic response in <5 min
success: 90% of inquiries handled without a human
03

Execution with quality control

Karolina (Scrum Master) monitors pace and procedures. 14 automated checkpoints verify quality at every step. Nothing gets overlooked.

Karolina Wieczorek — execution and quality control
04

Double verification

Technical Done — does the code work, do tests pass, are procedures met.
Business Done — was the INTENT achieved, was the client's problem solved. Only then does the task go to you for approval.

05

Learning and growth

After every task, knowledge is saved to the team's memory. Best practices are promoted to the company-wide knowledge base. The system gets smarter with every sprint — and never forgets.

"This is not SCRUM, nor an AI system. It's a system that combines: execution + memory + feedback + human decisions."
